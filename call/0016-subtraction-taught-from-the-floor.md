# Subtraction taught from the floor, and named in the budget

- Status: accepted
- Scope: cathach
- Date: 2026-07-07

## Context and Problem Statement

The hand tier's repair is subtraction: a located erasure is filled by subtracting the
total of the readable digits from the guard S. Yet the budget recorded in call/0008
names addition and multiplication, call/0013 closed the taught-never-assumed breach
for multiplication alone, and no draft teaches subtraction from the floor. The two
working drafts disagree in their openings about whether subtraction is in the budget
at all, and one asserts of the repair that "it asks no more than you already know",
which states the assumption outright. The borrow case, where the digit to remove is
larger than the guard, is half-taught in one draft and absent from the other. The
cast's humanist review surfaced the gap
(plan/0001-durable-book-format/humanist-review.md).

## Decision

Subtraction is named in the budget and taught from the tally floor, before the first
repair figure uses it. The budget's arithmetic now includes subtraction beside the
addition and multiplication already named.

The teaching form matches the floor: cross out as many strokes as the count to
remove, then count the strokes that stand. The borrow case is taught with it: when
the digit to remove is larger than the guard, one full enclosure is added to the
guard first, as Nuala's draft already shows for the base-ten case.

This amends call/0013 the way call/0013 amended call/0008. The floor stays at
counting, and nothing about numbers is assumed.

## Consequences

- The `#draft-preamble` task teaches subtraction from strokes before
  repair-by-subtraction appears, and both working drafts inherit the fix when their
  figures regenerate against the settled base.
- The budget statements in the drafts' openings are aligned at regeneration, so every
  opening names the same taught set: counting, addition, subtraction, and
  multiplication.
- The floor of call/0008 is unchanged.
