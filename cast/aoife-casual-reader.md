# Aoife: the Casual Reader

*The visitor who points a phone at a beautiful book.*

**Modality: mobile and momentary.** Aoife meets a cathach book as a
coffee-table object, a beautiful printed volume of a subject's work sitting on a table.
She is curious but unhurried, carries only an iPhone, and reaches for Safari and the
camera before any app or manual. She will scan a page to see what it holds, and she
will lose interest the instant it asks for effort or setup. She is not preserving
anything; she is enjoying it.

- **Goals:** point her phone camera at a page and see the content it encodes; cross
  between the printed object and its digital contents with nothing installed; share
  what she finds; enjoy the book as an object as much as its payload.
- **Frustrations:** anything that needs an app, an account, or a manual; a scan that is
  slow or fiddly; a page that reads as noise rather than a designed object; a result
  she cannot share or return to.
- **Works by:** opening the camera or Safari, framing a page, and letting a
  browser-based decoder reveal the content on the spot.
