# Oisín: the Far-Future Hand-Decoder

*The reader who arrives after the machines are gone.*

**Modality: unaided and post-technological.** Oisín holds a cathach book centuries
after it was printed. He has no computer and no surviving software, no scanner, only
the printed preamble, a pen, paper, and whatever a literate person is presumed to
know. He reads slowly and checks his arithmetic by hand. He cannot run a program, yet
he can add, he can multiply, and he can follow written instructions. Everything he
recovers, he recovers by eye and by hand.

- **Goals:** learn the format from the printed preamble alone; recover a chosen region
  exactly, within a budget of addition and multiplication; repair local damage by
  subtraction; rebuild enough of the tool tier to let a machine finish the rest.
- **Frustrations:** instructions that assume knowledge he does not have; a step that
  needs division or a modular inverse; glyphs too close to tell apart once damaged; a
  digest no human can compute on paper.
- **Works by:** reading the plain instructional register, transcribing glyphs, running
  hand-checkable sums, and recovering a located erasure one symbol at a time.
