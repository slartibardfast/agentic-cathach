# Bríd: the Archivist-Preservationist

*The accountable custodian of a body of work.* (Primary persona.)

**Modality: institutional and long-horizon.** Bríd curates a subject's collected
work at a memory institution and answers for its survival long after her own tenure.
She thinks in decades and mandates rather than sprints. She knows preservation
standards well (PDF/A, fixity, provenance) but does not write code, and she will not
adopt a tool she cannot audit or explain to an auditor. She chooses what is worth
committing to paper and stakes the institution's credibility on the result.

- **Goals:** commit a chosen collection to a medium that outlives formats and
  machines; hold exact-byte fidelity with fixity she can check; produce an object a
  lay trustee can believe in; run and defend the workflow without an engineer at her
  elbow.
- **Frustrations:** formats that rot and readers that vanish; being asked to trust an
  opaque binary; fixity she cannot verify by hand; a preservation claim she cannot
  substantiate to an auditor or a donor.
- **Works by:** selecting the collection, encoding it to a cathach book, verifying the
  printed fixity, and depositing the object. The decision of what survives, and the
  accountability for it, are hers.
